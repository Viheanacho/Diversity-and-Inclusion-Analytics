# Diversity-and-Inclusion-Analytics
This project was carried out to provide a data-driven evaluation of organizational equity and representation. By leveraging an open-source dataset, a comprehensive analytical framework was constructed to monitor demographic trends and identify potential biases within the corporate lifecycle.
Project Overview:
The primary objective of this analysis was the transformation of workforce data into actionable insights regarding Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI). A multi-dimensional approach was adopted to ensure that representation was assessed not only by headcount but through the lens of career progression and retention.
Key Analytical Components:
The following areas were prioritized and visualized within the dashboard:
•	Executive Gender Balance: The distribution of gender across senior leadership roles was scrutinized to assess the "glass ceiling" effect and top-level representation.
•	Hiring Trends: Recruitment data was analysed to determine the inclusivity of the intake pipeline and the effectiveness of diverse sourcing strategies.
•	Promotion Equity: The frequency and velocity of promotions were compared across demographic groups to identify disparities in career advancement.
•	Performance Rating Analysis: Internal appraisals were evaluated to ensure that performance scores were distributed equitably and were free from systemic bias.
•	Turnover Rate: Attrition patterns were monitored to identify if specific minority groups departed the organization at disproportionate rates.
Methodology:
1.	Data Acquisition: An open-source HR dataset containing demographic identifiers, job levels, and performance history was sourced.
2.	Data Cleaning and Transformation: Data was standardized to ensure consistent categorization of gender, ethnicity, and department metrics.
3.	Comparative Analysis: Statistical benchmarks were established to compare the representation of protected groups against the overall organizational average.
4.	Visual Synthesis: An interactive dashboard was designed, allowing stakeholders to filter insights by department, region, and Age group.
Conclusion:
Through this project, it was demonstrated that diversity is a metric of both recruitment and retention. By visualizing the correlation between performance ratings and promotion rates, a clear path toward a more equitable corporate culture was established, and areas requiring immediate intervention were highlighted.
Project Specifications
•	Data Source: Open-source Diversity and Inclusion Dataset
•	Focus Areas: Gender Representation, Promotion Parity, Attrition Analysis
